# Level Framework for Teaching the Arabic Language

**From Level A1 to Level C2**

Aligned with the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR)
Six levels • More than 30 teaching units per level

> Source language: Egyptian Colloquial Arabic (Egyptian dialect / ʿāmmiyya).

## Level Index

| Level | Name | Number of Units |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| A1 | Beginner (Breakthrough) | 25 units + placement/assessment exam |
| A2 | Lower Intermediate (Waystage) | 32 units |
| B1 | Intermediate (Threshold) | 32 units |
| B2 | Upper Intermediate (Vantage) | 31 units |
| C1 | Effective Operational Proficiency | 31 units |
| C2 | Mastery | 31 units |

## Important Notes

1. Egyptian colloquial Arabic is not bound by all the rules of Standard Arabic; there is no case inflection (*iʿrāb*) and no grammatical case markings in everyday speech.
2. Word endings do not change for grammatical case.
3. Some letters are pronounced differently (e.g., *qāf* is usually pronounced as a glottal stop / *alif*: *qalam* → *ʔalam*, *qamar* → *ʔamar*; and *thāʼ* and *dhāl* are sometimes shifted to *tāʼ* or *dāl*: *thaʿlab* → *taʿlab*, *dharra* → *durra*).
4. Some words in the colloquial differ from Standard Arabic (e.g., *al-ʾān* "now" → *dilwaʔti*; *mādhā* "what" → *ēh*; *dhahaba* "went" → *rāḥ*).
5. Sentence order in the colloquial is more flexible, so word order is not strict.
6. Present-tense verbs in the colloquial usually begin with the letter *bā* (b-), e.g., (*Ahmed goes to work* → *Aḥmad biyrūḥ il-shughl*; *she cooks* → *bitṭbukh*; *he plays* → *biyilʿab*; *he eats* → *biyākul*; *she runs* → *bitigri*).
7. The future tense begins with *hā* or *ḥā* (ha-): *I will go* → *harūḥ*.
8. Egyptian colloquial Arabic is a spoken language and is generally not written in books, magazines, or newspapers except rarely (and if used in writing, the reader tends to consider it a sign of weak command of Arabic by the writer).

## Level A1 — Beginner

This level represents the learner's starting point in Arabic, focusing on building the phonetic and writing foundation and the basic vocabulary of daily life.

### Learning Objectives

- Recognize the Arabic alphabet, its sounds, and its letter shapes at the beginning, middle, and end of words.
- Acquire basic vocabulary covering immediate daily needs (introductions, family, numbers, colors, time).
- Build simple sentences for everyday use.
- Develop accurate pronunciation of letters and their points of articulation.
- Be able to understand very simple familiar phrases related to satisfying concrete needs.

### Learning Outcomes

- Reads and writes the Arabic letters in their various forms accurately.
- Introduces themselves, asks for and answers simple personal information.
- Understands and uses familiar everyday expressions and very simple basic phrases.
- Interacts in a simple way provided the other person speaks slowly and clearly.
- Writes a short postcard or fills out a form with basic personal information.

| 3 | Colloquial Independent Pronouns | *ana, inta, inti, huwwa, hiyya, iḥna, humma*... and their use in simple introductory sentences. |
| 4 | Colloquial Demonstratives | *da, di, dōl*, and their use in pointing to people and things. |
| 5 | Numbers from 1 to 10 | Pronouncing and writing numbers and using them in simple counting and prices. |

## Level A2 — Lower Intermediate

This level builds on the basics of A1, expanding the learner's ability to communicate in routine everyday situations and exchange direct information about familiar topics.

### Learning Objectives

- Expand the lexicon to cover broader areas of daily life (travel, work, services).
- Master the conjugation of past and present verbs with all pronouns.
- Be able to describe experiences, past events, and near-future plans in a simplified manner.
- Develop the skill of interacting in short routine dialogues requiring a direct exchange of information.

### Learning Outcomes

- Communicates in simple, direct tasks requiring an exchange of information about familiar topics.
- Describes, in simple terms, aspects of their background, immediate environment, and immediate needs.
- Understands commonly used sentences and phrases related to areas of immediate priority.
- Writes short messages and simple notes in partially connected form.
- Handles routine social situations such as shopping, travel, and making inquiries.

## Level B1 — Intermediate

This level aims to enable the learner to handle most situations while traveling or working, and to express their opinions and experiences in a connected, coherent way.

### Learning Objectives

- Develop the ability to produce coherent texts on familiar topics or topics of personal interest.
- Acquire the skill of understanding the main ideas in clear texts in simplified colloquial language.
- Develop the ability to participate in dialogues on general topics without prior preparation.
- Be able to justify opinions and briefly explain plans, giving reasons and explanations.

### Learning Outcomes

- Understands the main points of clear input on familiar topics in work, school, and leisure.
- Handles most situations that may arise while traveling in Egypt.
- Produces simple, connected text on familiar topics or topics of personal interest.
- Describes experiences, events, dreams, hopes, and ambitions, and gives brief reasons for opinions and plans.
- Writes a report or a short, organized letter that presents information and ideas clearly.

## Level B2 — Upper Intermediate

This level focuses on deepening the understanding of Egyptian life and the customs and traditions of the Egyptian people on various occasions, and on developing fluency in spontaneous interaction with speakers of the colloquial language without strain across many topics.

### Learning Objectives

- Develop the ability to understand more specialized texts on topics specific to the Egyptian environment.
- Enable the learner to interact fluently and spontaneously with native speakers.
- Refine the skill of explaining different points of view on contemporary issues.
- Learn the particularities of the Egyptian environment — social life, antiquities, and more.

### Learning Outcomes

- Understands the main ideas, including technical discussions in their field.
- Interacts fluently and spontaneously to a degree that makes regular communication with native speakers possible without strain for either party.
- Produces clear, detailed text on a wide range of topics, and explains their viewpoint on a particular issue, outlining the advantages and disadvantages of different options.
- Analyzes and comments on others' opinions, and presents logical arguments supported by examples.

## Level C1 — Advanced

This level aims to enable the learner to use the colloquial language flexibly and effectively for more specialized purposes and topics, whether social, academic, or professional.

### Learning Objectives

- Master colloquial language in advanced topics and apply it in oral speech.
- Develop the ability to understand a wide range of long texts and grasp their implicit meanings.
- Enable the learner to express themselves fluently and spontaneously without obvious searching for suitable expressions.
- Refine the skill of using the language flexibly and effectively for social, academic, professional, and specialized purposes and topics.

### Learning Outcomes

- Understands a wide range of long and demanding texts, and grasps their implicit meanings.
- Expresses themselves fluently and spontaneously without obvious searching for suitable expressions.
- Uses the language flexibly and effectively for different purposes (social, academic, professional, political, and others).

## Level C2 — Fluency / Mastery

This level represents the peak of linguistic proficiency, where the learner becomes able to understand almost everything they read or hear with ease, and to express themselves with complete accuracy and spontaneity approaching the proficiency of an educated native speaker.

### Learning Objectives

- Reach the ability to understand almost everything read or heard with complete ease and with virtually no effort.
- Master distinguishing fine semantic nuances in different situations (sarcasm / criticism / humor).
- Develop the ability to summarize information and reformulate it in a coherent presentation.
- Refine the ability for spontaneous expression with high accuracy.

### Learning Outcomes

- Understands with complete ease almost everything they hear or read, whether spoken directly or broadcast.
- Summarizes information from different spoken and written sources, reconstructing arguments and accounts in a coherent presentation.
- Expresses themselves spontaneously and with high accuracy, distinguishing fine shades of meaning even in the most complex situations.
- Analyzes specialized heritage, literary, and academic texts and discusses them critically and appreciatively at an advanced level.
